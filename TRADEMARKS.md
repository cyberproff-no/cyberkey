# CyberKey and CyberProff Trademark Policy

## Scope

This policy applies to the following names and visual identifiers:

- CyberKey
- CyberKey Slim
- CyberProff
- CyberProff.no
- Associated logos
- Product names
- Visual identity
- Domain names and related branding

These identifiers are not licensed under the GNU General Public License
or any other open-source license in this repository.

## Permitted use

You may use the CyberKey and CyberProff names when necessary to:

- Identify the original CyberKey project.
- Describe an unmodified copy of CyberKey.
- State that a fork or derivative is based on CyberKey.
- Meet GPL attribution and source-notice obligations.

Such use must be factual and must not imply that CyberProff.no endorses,
supports, certifies, sponsors, or is affiliated with your project,
product, organization, or service.

## Restricted use

You may not use CyberKey, CyberKey Slim, CyberProff, CyberProff.no, or
associated branding in a way that could cause confusion about origin,
ownership, endorsement, certification, sponsorship, or support.

Examples include:

- Naming a fork or derivative product "CyberKey".
- Using CyberKey or CyberProff logos in a derivative product.
- Marketing a modified version as an official CyberKey release.
- Selling products or services using CyberKey or CyberProff branding
  without written permission.
- Registering domains, usernames, package names, or social-media accounts
  that create confusion with CyberKey or CyberProff.

## Forks and derivative works

Forks and derivative works must use a clearly distinct name and visual
identity.

A derivative project may state:

> Based on CyberKey, originally developed by CyberProff.no.

It must not state or imply:

> Official CyberKey release.

unless written permission has been granted by CyberProff.no.
